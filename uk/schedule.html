Keynote: Irina Bolychevsky

The information age is transforming how we work, live and interact. We are all increasingly dependent on digital services to maintain our public identity. However, instead of optimising for our needs these services ultimately succeed if they can 'encourage' us to buy or sign up to products and services we didn't know we wanted or needed. Sometimes this can be useful, but many times it's exploitative and manipulative and chips away at the trust people place in the internet and Web.

How can we make openness and decentralisation the default? What are some of the projects leading the charge and what challenges should we be tackling together?

Irina Bolychevsky is the founder of redecentralize.org where she explores some of the implications of centralisation, the projects working to change this, and the opptunities and challenges of decentralisation.
